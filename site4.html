VALUES, BELIEFS & PERSONAL PREDICTIONS

My life, but also my goals and research, are based on certain values. They serve as a reference and evaluation of my work. This page lists some of my most important values and views. Moreover, you can read some of my predictions for the future.

1) Sustainability

The term "sustainability" comprises three dimensions: ecological, economic and social sustainability. Personally, I try above all to live in an ecologically and socially (see section "Philantropy") sustainable way.

For me, environmentally friendly living means limited travelling (if necessary by public transport), low and environmentally conscious consumption, reducing electricity consumption to a minimum and separating and recycling waste. I also support animal welfare organisations, such as WWF, and actively research technologies that can solve our current environmental problems. As an environmentally conscious student, I am very concerned about the current developments in global environmental policy and the state of essential ecosystems. Climate change is causing irreversible changes for all living organisms and increasing global inequality. I am also very worried about the extinction of species and environmental pollution. For example, I investigated the production of antibiotics in India and found out about the terrible consequences that are already having an impact today.

2) Philantropy

In order to develop my research in a socially sustainable way, I try to use common, inexpensive and commercially available materials. Furthermore, I avoid patents and focus on the democratisation of designs and manufacturing processes.

My ultimate goal is to commercialise my sustainable technologies. Once I have the necessary financial capacity, I will use all my assets as efficiently as possible for philanthropic purposes. My goal in life is to fight poverty and eventually eradicate it. As a moral person, I think that poverty is unacceptable given the extreme wealth in the West. Our empathy should not be limited to ethnicity or borders. As long as someone is suffering while I can enjoy life, I have an unrelenting urge to fix the injustice.

3) Cosmopolitanism

Because of my multicultural and multilingual socialization, various languages and cultures are also in my field of knowledge and have led to cosmopolitan thinking. I had the privilege of travelling to various countries in Europe as a young teenager. This allowed me to understand different perspectives and enjoy the food, architecture, art and landscapes of different nations.

But it's also important to mention that since I became aware of climate change, I've only travelled to visit my family or for professional reasons. That's why many of the pictures below were taken by my family. Nevertheless, they are proof of my family's cultural openness and way of thinking, which have influenced me.

Prediction #1: The new era: Symbiocene

The Symbiocene, represents a period where humanity integrates symbiotic principles into social life, reconnecting with nature. Important principles for the Symbiocene include eliminating toxic substances, prioritizing renewable resources, and repairing symbiotic bonds. These principles are deemed feasible and already in practice in emerging innovations such as biodegradable materials and renewable energy sources.

The term "Symbiocene" was coined by Glenn Albrecht. Glenn Albrecht's text, "An Invitation to the Symbiocene," addresses the challenges of the Anthropocene era, characterized by human dominance causing environmental crises like climate change, extinction, and pollution. Albrecht emphasizes the urgent need to change direction due to the negative impacts affecting both Earth's ecosystems and human well-being. The Anthropocene era evolved with flawed ideologies, leading to a globalized, homogenized system detrimental to life. Albrecht proposes the Symbiocene as a new era fostering symbiotic thinking for nurturing humanity alongside nature. The concept of symbiosis, indicating organisms living together for mutual benefit, is central. Scientific discoveries reveal the importance of symbiosis from micro to macro levels, emphasizing the interconnectedness of life. Albrecht challenges the perception of ourselves, considering humans as holobionts, composed of multiple organisms coexisting. Albrecht critiques the concept of 'the environment', advocating for a shift towards a sumbiocentric view acknowledging the interconnectedness of life. Albrecht envisions the emergence of a biofilm marking the geological commencement of the Symbiocene, covering past pollution. Ultimately, the Symbiocene offers guidance for a sustainable future based on symbiotic relationships and a swift transition.

Furthermore, people will live in small, self-sufficient, and anarchistic communities (deglobalisation, local economies).

Prediction #2: Transhumanism

Due to many rapidly emergent technologies, like artificial intelligence (ChatGPT), biotechnology, nantotechnology and genetic engineering, I believe that we are going to enhance our human capabilities and transcend the limitations of the human body and mind. We will improve our human physical, cognitive, and psychological abilities. I envision a future where we overcome aging, disease, and other biological constraints through technological advancements. The first cyborgs live already among us. In near future, as we better understand our brains, we will have brain-computer interfaces (e.g. Neuralink), which will revolutionise the way we communicate, learn and work.

Prediction #3: World government

Once Mars colonisations are established and humanity becomes a multiplanetary or eventually a multistellar species, conflicts will shift from the nation level to the planet level. Moreover, as the Internet and other future networks will have made the younger generations more or less culturally and ideologically homogeneous, religious and national identities will lose their value. This will result in a supranational unification movement. Borders and national governments will disappear. One world government will rule over all of humanity on earth. Inequality on earth will decrease as the well-being of each individual (national interests have disappeared) will be a priority.

Prediction #4: Labour-free society

The cost of producing goods will converge towards zero in the future with increasing automation. Robots and machines will take over all our physical tasks. Our current understanding of the economy and money will no longer make sense. The only currency will be creativity and humanness. Going to work will be voluntary.
